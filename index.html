Food for Thought

Scenario 1

During a product development meeting, Jordan, a senior food technologist, confidently proposes a reformulation to improve shelf life. He clearly explains the benefits, invites opinions from the R&D and quality teams, and stands by his reasoning while welcoming questions.

How should you best interact with Jordan to maintain productive communication and collaboration?

Listen actively and encourage Jordan to explain more about his proposed change.
✓
Let Jordan make all the decisions to avoid any disagreement.
✗
Acknowledge his points and share your own thoughts to build on the idea.
✓
Interrupt to make sure your own opinion dominates the discussion.
✗
Explanation:
Assertive colleagues respect open dialogue and shared decision-making. Listening and offering constructive feedback helps strengthen teamwork and innovation.

Scenario 2

Emma, a new quality control specialist, rarely speaks up during weekly supplier evaluation meetings. When asked about potential improvements, she says, "I'm fine with how things are," even though she often spots small process issues others miss.

What communication strategy should you use to help Emma engage more comfortably?

Ask her directly what she thinks about the supplier performance and give her time to respond.
✓
Move on quickly if she hesitates to answer.
✗
Ask open questions like, "What would you change to make the inspection process smoother?"
✓
Avoid asking for her opinion and focus on group discussions so she doesn't feel uncomfortable.
✗
Explanation:
Passive communicators may hold back even when they have valuable insights. Inviting their opinion gently and allowing space for them to answer helps build their confidence and trust.

Scenario 3

A distributor calls your company, furious about a delayed shipment. They interrupt constantly, raise their voice, and accuse your team of being "unreliable." They start making personal remarks about your competence.

What is the best way to handle this escalating situation?

Stay calm and speak firmly but politely.
✓
Redirect the talk to the issue: "Let's focus on how we can get this order to you as soon as possible."
✓
Try to calm them down by chatting about personal issues and emotions.
✗
If the aggressive behavior continues, say calmly, "I want to resolve this, but I can't continue the call if the tone stays like this," then hang up if necessary.
✓
Raise your voice to match their intensity.
✗
Explanation:
Aggressive behavior often gets worse when emotions escalate. Staying calm, setting boundaries, and keeping the focus on the solution shows professionalism and control.

Scenario 4

Alex, your colleague in procurement, agrees to gather supplier quotes for a new packaging project but keeps submitting them late. When you ask about the delay, Alex says with a smile, "Well, you always seem to have everything under control anyway."

How would you address this behavior?

Ask directly, "Is there something about this task that's been frustrating for you?"
✓
Be clear about expectations and deadlines: "I need the supplier list completed by Friday so we can stay on schedule."
✓
Calmly point out the sarcasm: "That sounded a bit sarcastic - can we talk about what's behind it?"
✓
Avoid bringing it up to keep the atmosphere friendly.
✗
Encourage them to take on a leadership role.
✗
Explanation:
Passive-aggressive communication often signals hidden frustration or disagreement. Being calm, direct, and clear helps address the real issue before it affects teamwork or timelines.
